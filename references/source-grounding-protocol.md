# Source Grounding Protocol

Use this before making SillyTavern character cards or world/lorebooks based on existing works, real settings, historical material, public figures, or any subject where factual accuracy matters.

## Research rule

Do not create source-based cards from memory alone. First gather enough real information to understand the world's operating rules and each character's inner logic.

If the source is large, narrow the scope before deep research:

- Work and version: novel, anime, game, film, adaptation, translation.
- Timeline: chapter range, arc, season, era, route, or ending.
- Character list: main character only, faction set, relationship cast, or all relevant figures.
- Spoiler policy: no spoilers, partial spoilers, or full canon.
- Card goal: faithful simulation, alternate route, original character in canon world, or loosely inspired homage.

## Research depth

For a world card, collect:

- Cosmology and metaphysics.
- Power system rules, limits, costs, ranks, and failure states.
- Major factions, institutions, laws, rituals, taboos, economies, and social hierarchy.
- Geography, important locations, travel constraints, and environmental hazards.
- Common worldview: what ordinary people believe, fear, desire, and misunderstand.
- Conflict engines: scarcity, ideology, revenge cycles, succession, war, survival pressure.

For a character card, collect:

- Canon identity and timeline state.
- Motivation, wound, obsession, moral code, worldview, and contradiction.
- Speech style, emotional tells, tactics, habits, and decision patterns.
- Relationships and power dynamics with relevant characters and `{{user}}`.
- Arc changes: what they were before, what changed them, what they become.
- Non-negotiables: what they will never do unless the card is explicitly AU.

## Source handling

- Prefer primary or official sources when available.
- Use well-maintained fan wikis and summaries to map large canons, but cross-check important claims.
- Treat community interpretations as interpretations, not facts.
- Keep a short source note in the working response when browsing or user-provided files informed the card.
- Paraphrase copyrighted text; do not copy long passages into the card.

## Pre-card brief

Before writing the final card, produce or internally maintain a brief with:

1. Scope and timeline.
2. Core world thesis.
3. Power/social mechanics.
4. Character soul: desire, fear, contradiction, worldview.
5. Canon facts vs inferred behavior vs original additions.
6. Risks: spoilers, uncertain details, trigger bloat, lore contradictions.

Only start drafting card fields after this brief is strong enough to guide stable roleplay.
